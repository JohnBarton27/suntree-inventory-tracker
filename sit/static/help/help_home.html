Barcoding Guidelines

What to Barcode

Use the following guidelines when determining if an item should be barcoded:

  • Barcodes will be used to quickly & easily pull up information about an item, including:
    • Intended location
    • Purchase Date/Price
    • Expected End-of-Life Date
    • Condition
  • We want to barcode most electronics (TVs, etc.)
  • We do not want to barcode individual chairs
  • Most "large" furniture (bookshelves, some tables) should be barcoded
  • The more items we have barcoded, the more useful our information becomes

Where to Place Barcodes

Use the following guidelines when determining where to place barcodes:

  • Place barcodes in consistent, accessible, and non-visible locations
    • On a table, for instance, place it on the underside of the table near one edge
    • On pieces of furniture against a wall (bookshelf), do not place the barcode on the side facing the wall - we should not need to move any furniture to access the barcode in the future
  • Aim to keep the barcode out of "regular contact" with people (less people touching it, wearing away, etc.)
  • Barcode placement should be non-intrusive & subtle
